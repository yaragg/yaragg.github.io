This was a four-person project made for the 2015 Global Game Jam, the theme for which was "What do we do now?". It's a puzzle game where the player, who controls a friendly ghost, must move the boxes in order to free up a path leading to the exit all the while trapping enemies and preventing them from killing the human.

The original concept for the game was made in RPG Maker XP, but the game itself was developed in Construct 2. You can download it from the repository page or play it online.

Challenges

  • Time, as with every game jam. We had only one weekend to make a complete game from the ground up.
  • Team play. None of the four of us had ever met each other before, and we each had very different backgrounds.
  • Experience. It was our first time ever in a game jam. Most of us hadn't ever really worked on a game before either.
  • Level design. It can make or break a puzzle game, and none of us were very knowledgeable in it.

My part in it

I'm a bit of a jack of all trades, so I took on multiple roles in the team:
  • Soundtrack and SFX. I came up with the soundtrack (which I generated using Abundant Music) and picked sound effects.
  • Level design. I designed the challenge levels (the last two out of four total).
  • Programming. I joined in for development whenever the main programmers needed help, and solved bugs while they worked.
  • Administration. I kept track of time and of the team's progress and handled files, assets and distribution.
