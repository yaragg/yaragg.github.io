Requirements and notes

Requirements:
  • fancybox gallery is in all project pages
  • jQuery transitions are in the Skills page, when moving from one section to the other
  • DHTML used in Skills page to hide and show content
  • Form and FormMail in Contact page
  • SSI for including headers and footers in every page
  • SSI for 'last updated' indicator in Projects page
  • .htaccess 404 page and password protected pages linked above
Note:
  • I purposely used the JPEG format for screenshots even when the flat colors would have been better suited for PNG. The file size was getting big and I was afraid of running out of space on Gibson.
  • For the first index page, where there's no content and you just choose the language, I didn't use SSI because there is no navigation and the flags are styled differently.
  • The print stylesheet doesn't validate because of the attr(href) bit. Apparently attr() is a feature that is at risk of being dropped as per the October 25th draft of the W3C recommended CSS values, so I'm guessing that's why. I decided to leave it in anyway as it makes maintaining the portfolio easier, as opposed to having to manually add links with a print-only class every time I use a link in a page.

Challenges

I'm actually surprised at how easy this felt compared to the tutorial website. Everything just seemed to go more smoothly, probably because of the experience acquired during the first project.

The most difficult part was actually coming up with the content. My home institution (University of SÃ£o Paulo) seems to favor more academic programming assignments such as determining whether an N-dimensional graph is connected as opposed to creative works. Since I want to work in game development, I need mostly creative works in my portfolio, so I struggled to find things to fulfill the 7 page requirement.

Coding wise, I would say the most difficult parts were making the navbar stay centered and making the thumbnails and their mouseovers. Centering content actually seems to be a recurring problem for me...

Final thoughts

I started out with pretty much no idea what I wanted to make this site look like. At first I was going to try something similar to my tutorial website except with a more fantasy-like color scheme, but I decided that I wanted to try a top navbar instead of a side one. Once I recalled I could use transparency for the section backgrounds, I had the idea to use a cool background image, and the rest of the design sort of evolved from that.

All in all I'm really happy with this website! I like the look of it, though I might be biased since I really like purple. I'm also glad I thought of translating the pages--since I live in Brazil, having a multilingual portfolio is definitely a plus.

There are two things I think I could have improved. The logo was added only after I'd finished the layout and design, and I'm still not very satisfied with where it's positioned. I don't know where to put it though. I also don't like how the project thumbnails leave some blank space to the right until there's enough space to add another thumbnail, it looks weird, but I haven't been able to solve this problem.
